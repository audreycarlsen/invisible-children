First in the series: the invisible children
Problem
Washington’s child welfare system is mostly closed to adolescents and teenagers. When youths can no longer stay at home and aren’t placed in foster care, they often land on the street, where they are easily victimized.
Solution
The Legislature should reopen the door to the child welfare system for these youths with renewed investment in family preservation services and other "upstream" services.

Editor's note
Editorial writer Jonathan Martin spent several months looking at youth homelessness from all angles, traveling in-state and out to talk with kids, parents, foster parents, social workers, state officials and lawmakers. The result is a series of editorials sharing his findings and suggested remedies. Also included in this examination are guest columns.
